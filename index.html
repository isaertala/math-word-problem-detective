🗝️ Keyword Clue Bank

+ Addition Clues

These words mean put together, so ADD.

altogether in all total sum combined in total both
Example: Sam has 4 red balloons and 5 blue balloons. How many balloons does he have in total?
4 + 5 = 9 balloons

− Subtraction Clues

These words mean take away or compare, so SUBTRACT.

left fewer difference how many more remain gave away less than
Example: There are 10 birds on a branch. 4 birds fly away. How many birds are left?
10 − 4 = 6 birds

× Multiplication Clues

These words mean equal groups joining, so MULTIPLY.

each every per times groups of twice double
Example: There are 4 pages of stickers. Each page has 3 stickers. How many stickers in all?
4 × 3 = 12 stickers

÷ Division Clues

These words mean splitting into equal groups, so DIVIDE.

share equally split each group divided by equally among how many in each
Example: 15 cookies are shared equally among 5 friends. How many cookies does each friend get?
15 ÷ 5 = 3 cookies

⚠ Watch Out! Two-Step Clues

Sometimes a keyword means there's a HIDDEN step. You must work out one thing FIRST, then use that answer to solve the real question.

more than less than than after then combined age left after buying both
Example: Tom has 5 apples. Mia has 3 more apples than Tom. How many apples do they have altogether?
Step 1 (hidden step): Mia's apples = 5 + 3 = 8
Step 2 (real question): 5 + 8 = 13 apples altogether

📦 Container Clues

Watch for boxes, baskets, bags, jars, or groups holding equal amounts — they're often a multiplication or division clue! If you know how many are in EACH container and how many containers, MULTIPLY. If you know the TOTAL and how many containers, DIVIDE to find how many in each.

box / boxes basket / baskets bag / bags jar / jars container group / groups package / pack crate
Example: There are 3 baskets. Each basket has 6 oranges. How many oranges are there in total?
You know the amount in EACH basket, so MULTIPLY: 3 × 6 = 18 oranges
🕵️ A detective's reminder: keywords are helpful clues, but they're not a magic trick! Sometimes a word like "more" can still mean subtraction, depending on the story. Always read the WHOLE problem and think about what is actually happening — the keyword just gives you a hint, it doesn't decide the answer for you.

CASE FILE #2

Liam has 6 toy cars. His brother gives him 5 more toy cars. How many toy cars does Liam have in all?

The keyword is "in all" — that means put the groups together. Which operation is that?
6 + 5 = 11. Liam has 11 toy cars in all. CASE CLOSED

CASE FILE #3

There are 14 birds on a fence. 9 birds fly away. How many birds are left on the fence?

The keyword is "left" — some birds flew away, so the group got smaller.
14 − 9 = 5. There are 5 birds left on the fence. CASE CLOSED

CASE FILE #4

Ana picked 7 red apples and 8 green apples. How many apples did she pick altogether?

The keyword is "altogether" — two groups of apples are joining together.
7 + 8 = 15. Ana picked 15 apples altogether. CASE CLOSED

CASE FILE #5

Each shelf has 5 books. There are 3 shelves. How many books are there in all?

The keyword is "Each" — equal groups joining together means MULTIPLY.
5 × 3 = 15. There are 15 books in all. CASE CLOSED

CASE FILE #6

18 marbles are shared equally among 3 friends. How many marbles does each friend get?

The keyword is "shared equally" — splitting into equal groups means DIVIDE.
18 ÷ 3 = 6. Each friend gets 6 marbles. CASE CLOSED

CASE FILE #7

Jack has 6 stamps. Mia has 4 more stamps than Jack. How many stamps do they have altogether?

The keyword is "more than" — that's a hidden step! First find Mia's stamps, THEN add both.
Step 1: Mia's stamps = 6 + 4 = 10. Step 2: 6 + 10 = 16. They have 16 stamps altogether. CASE CLOSED

CASE FILE #8

There are 4 boxes. Each box has 7 pencils. How many pencils are there in total?

The keywords are "boxes" and "Each" — you know the amount in each container, so MULTIPLY.
4 × 7 = 28. There are 28 pencils in total. CASE CLOSED
